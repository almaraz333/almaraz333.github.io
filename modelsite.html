Model Portfolio Site



A simple site to display high quality modeling photos for agencies and clients.




Project Overview


Challenge:

Design a way to best display a large amount of photos in a way that doesn't overwhelm the user. The photos need to easily accessible to featured on the front page prominently. This site would be the main way the client would get work so it was extremely important that the design let the client



Team:

Myself


The Process



What is the Goal?

To create and easy to use platform for the client to upload their content for agencies and potential employers to see. It was extremely important that this site be easily accessible by the client. My main concern was that the client be able to upload new pictures and edit their appearance on the site any time they want with no trouble. Managing content should not be a hindrance to the creation of content, it should facilitate it. Modeling was their passion and I felt an obligation to create something amazing for them to reflect their love of modeling.




Necessities:


  • Easy to use CMS
  • Sleek, minimalist design
  • Focus on the images
  • Accessible
  • Easily navigable IA
  • Simple UI


Booking

Bookings are the main way models generate income. It was essential that the user be able to find a way to contact client if they need to. To do this I made a simple page in which the user could send a message directly to the client's email with a pre-determined subject. This was an important part of the UX so the client can sort through their email and find only the ones relating to bookings. Being able to access data when you want it but having it out of the way when you don't is a cornerstone of a good user experience.




What I Learned

  1. Clear communication with the client is key to bringing their idea to life and creating the perfect product for them. Being a UX designer is more than just designing, it is connecting with other people.

  2. To design something means to know the user. Effectively creating a space means the designer must understand the user. This is not just asking simple "yes" or "no" questions. It is understanding WHAT the user is doing, WHY they are doing it, and HOW they are accomplishing that.

  3. Creating something so close to a person's passion is a huge responsibility. This means the designer must connect with the user and the client to understand them. This goes beyond asking WHAT, WHY, and HOW. The designer must empathize with the user and create a connection with them.

Deliverables

The final deliverable was a professional portfolio site for the client to display their content for potential future employment as a model.
